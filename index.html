My biography if you want to know me better

I was born and lived in Ukraine until February 24, 2022.
Since school time, namely in the 8th grade, my parents bought me a computer, and from this time began my journey into IT.
At school in computer science class, I met C++. And almost all my free time I wrote simple console applications for fun.
At the time of choosing a university, I clearly understood that I wanted to tie my professional career with programming.
I was enrolled in the National Technical University "Kharkiv Polytechnic Institute" at the Department of Energy at the Department of Automation and computer-integrated technologies.
At university, I continued to code in C++, but in my lectures, I was taught C#. And I fell in love with C#.
When I got my master's degree, I worked at Automation Spirit Alliance company where I was doing industrial process automation
and my duties included software design based on the documentation from the design department and writing the same software based on Siemens s7-1500 controllers and SCADA WinCC.
I also had experience in writing solutions for industrial test bench with LabVIEW software. During the Automation Spirit Alliance, Siemens held training conferences on Node-RED, which could be used in a new version of the software from Siemens.
Node-RED is a graphical programming interface written in modules using JS based on Node.js. From that moment I got interested in JS language, which opened me to the world of Web Development.
And I decided to learn how to create backend web applications. I started with PHP because PHP was recommended on various forums as the easiest language to enter and understand MVC architecture.
During this period, I learned how to use MySQL Doctrine Nginx and phpMyAdmin. But during the study, I did not like the syntax of PHP because it is quite cumbersome. And my colleague advised me to look towards Node.js.
I quickly switched to JS code because in some ways these two languages were similar, and I just had to understand the differences.
After I had a solid understanding of Node.js, I decided to use Nest.js as my framework because it offers MVC and SOLID rules out of the box, unlike express, and it reminded me a bit of the familiar Symfony.
Next, I studied the basic principles of Nest.js under the mentorship of my colleague, who advised me of this framework and Node.js in general.
